# Competitor Battle Cards

Four one-page comparisons. Each card is written for fast reference during a live call, not for a marketing page. When a buyer drops a competitor name, flip to that card.

---

## vs. Lattice

**What Lattice is:** A cloud performance-management platform aimed at 100-1,500 person companies. Lattice sells review cycles, 1:1s, OKRs, engagement surveys, and growth plans as a bundle.

**List price:** Around $11 per user per month for the Performance tier. Bundles with Engagement and Grow push effective cost to $18-$24 per user per month. Annual minimums commonly around $4K.

**Where Lattice wins:** Dedicated customer success manager. Rich review-cycle tooling with calibration workflows. Polished UI. Recognized brand for HR buyers who have seen it at their last three jobs.

**Where Primer wins:**

- **Total cost.** A 250-person company running Lattice pays roughly $33K the first year and $165K over five years. Primer is a one-time $5K. The five-year delta is $160K.
- **Ownership.** Lattice owns the database; you rent access to your own employee data. With Primer the database is yours.
- **Framework vs. cycles.** Lattice is built around annual or semi-annual review cycles. Primer is built around live weighted metrics that managers and leaders agreed on together. Different philosophy, and buyers who are sick of review cycles are the right buyers for Primer.
- **No review-cycle pressure.** Lattice's value drops when a company decides it's done with formal reviews. Primer doesn't care either way.

**How to position in one sentence:** "Lattice is the right call if your HR team wants a polished review-cycle tool and has budget for a subscription. Primer is the right call if your leaders want to own their metrics and your CFO wants to stop paying rent on performance management."

**Landmines:**

- Do not get into a feature fight on 1:1 tooling. Lattice has more features there.
- If the buyer loves calibration workflows, Primer is not the answer yet. Be honest about it.

## vs. 15Five

**What 15Five is:** A manager-enablement platform built around weekly check-ins, pulse surveys, and a lighter review layer. Targets SMB and mid-market, typically sold to HR.

**List price:** The Perform tier runs about $10 per user per month. The Focus and Total Platform bundles reach $14-$16 per user per month.

**Where 15Five wins:** Weekly check-in workflow. Manager-coaching content. Pulse survey cadence. Cheap on paper compared to Lattice.

**Where Primer wins:**

- **Check-ins aren't the problem.** 15Five solves the "managers don't talk to their reports enough" problem. Primer solves a different one: nobody agrees on what the reports are supposed to be measured on in the first place. A buyer who has deployed 15Five and still feels lost is exactly the Primer buyer.
- **Cost at scale.** 15Five at $12 per user per month costs $36K a year for 250 people. Primer's $5K one-time payment pays for itself in under two months.
- **Data sovereignty.** 15Five stores employee sentiment data in its cloud. Primer stores nothing anywhere except on the customer's own servers.
- **Weighted composite scoring.** 15Five asks for qualitative weekly reflections. Primer produces a quantitative tier score every person can see.

**How to position in one sentence:** "15Five is a coaching layer on top of your existing metrics. Primer is how you figure out what those metrics should be in the first place, and it's cheaper forever."

**Landmines:**

- Don't claim Primer replaces weekly check-ins. It doesn't.
- Buyers who love 15Five's pulse surveys should keep them. Primer is not a survey tool.

## vs. Culture Amp

**What Culture Amp is:** An engagement and development platform with a performance module bolted on. Enterprise-leaning, often sold alongside a dedicated People Science consultant.

**List price:** Perform module runs $9-$12 per user per month as a starting point, commonly bundled with Engage to reach $18-$24 per user per month. Annual contracts only. Implementation fees of $5K-$15K are common.

**Where Culture Amp wins:** Deep engagement survey science. Benchmark data across companies. Academic credibility with CHROs. Good for organizations that want a "People Science" program.

**Where Primer wins:**

- **Primer is for the operators, Culture Amp is for the HR function.** The moment a CEO, COO, or ops leader sees Culture Amp's interface they feel like it's an HR tool they have to tolerate. Primer feels like an operations tool they actually want.
- **Price.** A 250-person company on Culture Amp's bundled plan spends north of $180K over five years once implementation and add-ons are in. Primer spends $5K. Total.
- **No benchmark trap.** Culture Amp's value proposition is "how do you compare to other companies?" Primer's value proposition is "how do you compare to your own agreed-upon standard?" Buyers who are tired of being told their scores are average will prefer Primer.
- **Self-hosted.** Culture Amp is cloud-only. Primer runs inside the customer's own network.

**How to position in one sentence:** "Culture Amp is the right call for a CHRO who wants engagement benchmarks and a People Science program. Primer is the right call for a COO or CEO who wants leaders to own their metrics and never see a vendor invoice again."

**Landmines:**

- Don't fight on engagement science. Primer does not do engagement surveys.
- If the buyer's board cares about industry benchmarks, Culture Amp might still be the right answer. Acknowledge it.

## vs. BambooHR

**What BambooHR is:** An HRIS (core HR, PTO, onboarding, payroll integrations) with an add-on Performance Management module. Targets 50-500 person companies that need an HRIS first.

**List price:** Core HR about $6 per user per month. Performance add-on about $5 per user per month on top. Annual minimums apply.

**Where BambooHR wins:** BambooHR is an HRIS and Primer is not. If a buyer needs PTO tracking, onboarding workflows, document storage, and org-chart management, BambooHR handles it. Primer does none of that.

**Where Primer wins:**

- **Different category.** The honest positioning is that Primer and BambooHR are not the same product. A company should keep BambooHR for HR admin and add Primer for performance and goal management. The $5K Primer purchase sits alongside BambooHR, not against it.
- **BambooHR's performance module is weak.** It's a light review tool bolted onto an HRIS. Buyers who are using it are usually using it because they already pay for BambooHR, not because they love it. They are the easiest Primer customers because they already agree the performance module isn't working.
- **No per-seat fee.** BambooHR's performance add-on scales with headcount. Primer is flat forever.

**How to position in one sentence:** "Keep BambooHR for HR admin and payroll integrations. Add Primer for the performance-and-goals layer that BambooHR's module has never done well. The five-year delta pays for itself in the first year."

**Landmines:**

- Don't try to replace BambooHR. It's not a fight Primer wins and it's not a fight it should try.
- If the buyer is happy with BambooHR's performance module, move on. They're not the right prospect.
