# Email Templates

Five sequences. Copy, swap the bracketed bits, send. They're written to sound like a person wrote them at their desk, because a person did.

---

## 1. Cold Outreach

### Version A — For an HR leader at a 100-500 person company

**Subject:** a cheaper way to run performance at [Company]

Hi [First name],

Short note because I know you get a lot of these. I run [Your firm / Your role] and I've been recommending a performance tool called Primer to clients your size. Two reasons it keeps coming up.

First, it's $5,000 once. Not per year. Not per seat. Once. For a company of [headcount] people, the five-year difference versus what a tool like Lattice or 15Five costs is around [$X]. I'm happy to send the exact number if you tell me which tool you're on today.

Second, it's self-hosted source code, which means your security team doesn't have to add another vendor to the audit list. Your data never leaves your environment.

Worth a 20-minute call next week?

[Your name]

## 5. Stall-Breaker (when a deal has gone quiet)

**Subject:** should I close the file on this one?

Hi [First name],

I know it's been a few weeks since we talked about Primer, and I haven't heard back. That usually means one of three things, and I'd rather ask than guess:

1. The timing isn't right and you want me to check back in a quarter.
2. Something about the product didn't fit and you've already decided to pass.
3. This got buried under other work and you just haven't had the 20 minutes to respond.

Any of those is fine. Just tell me which one so I know whether to follow up or close the file. No awkwardness either way.

## Usage notes

A few things about how these are written, in case you want to adapt them:

- **Short subject lines in lowercase.** They feel like a personal note, not marketing. Open rates are measurably better.
- **One ask per email.** Every email in the sequence ends with one clear question the buyer can answer in a sentence.
- **Numbers go in the body.** Buyers forward emails with numbers in them. They don't forward emails without.
- **No signature bloat.** Your name, your phone, done. Anything else looks like a template.
