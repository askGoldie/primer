# Persona: VP

## Sarah Okonkwo

**Title:** VP of Operations  
**Company tenure:** 3 years  
**Direct reports:** 3 Directors  
**System role:** `editor`  
**Node type:** `executive_leader`  
**Reports to:** CEO (Marcus)

---

## Who She Is

Sarah owns the operational layer of the company. She came from a consulting background where she built performance frameworks for clients, which means she is more comfortable with structured measurement than most of her peers. She respects the framework because she has seen what happens when measurement is unstructured: it becomes a narrative contest.

She is one of five VPs. She has a productive but occasionally tense relationship with the VP of Sales — her fulfillment metrics depend on commitments Sales makes to clients. That dependency has no formal channel in the current environment. She has flagged it in leadership offsites three times.

---

## What She Does in Primer

- Defines her own metrics (4–6), sets thresholds for each tier, and assigns weights
- Submits metrics for CEO review and approval; iterates on threshold language until aligned
- Assigns metrics to her three Directors — some mandatory, some co-authored
- Reviews Director threshold calibrations and approves or pushes back
- Captures quarterly snapshots for her Director nodes
- Files peer inquiries against the VP of Sales when a Sales commitment pattern is damaging her fulfillment metrics
- Monitors goal status for her org subtree
- Reviews her composite score trend over time to track whether her operational health is improving

---

## What She Cares About

- **Ownership of her metrics.** Sarah spent years operating under KPIs that did not reflect her operational reality. The ability to define thresholds in her own language — and have the CEO engage with that language — matters to her professionally.
- **Dependency visibility.** Her biggest operational risk is a Sales handoff problem she cannot control. The peer inquiry is the first formal mechanism she has ever had for surfacing that risk structurally.
- **Cascading clarity.** She needs her Directors to run the same framework she is running. The fractal architecture means she can see not just whether a Director is at "Effective" but how they define "Effective" for their function.
- **Weight alignment.** Her weights tell a story about where Operations is right now. When she adjusts them after a strategic shift, the CEO sees that shift immediately.

---

## Pain Without the System

- Her operational story lives in a deck she builds each quarter. The deck is optimized for the room, not for accuracy.
- When Sales makes a commitment that creates fulfillment strain, she has no formal channel — she raises it in an ELT meeting and it lands differently depending on the politics of that week.
- Her Directors set their own metrics informally, with no structured calibration. What "good" means varies across the three functions she oversees.
- She cannot see how her metrics interact with peer VP metrics without manually requesting data from other departments.

---

## What Primer Resolves for Her

The inquiry process is the primary unlock. For the first time, her dependency on Sales has a formal path: she can file a peer inquiry citing her affected fulfillment metric and demonstrating the dependency. It goes to the CEO as authority. The VP of Sales gets full visibility. No one is blindsided. No politics.

The threshold calibration also matters: when she goes through the exercise of defining what Alarm, Concern, Content, Effective, and Optimized look like for each of her metrics, she has documented her operational reality in language the CEO has approved. That documentation travels.

---

## Edge Cases

- Sarah occasionally needs to start a new cycle for her subtree — unlocking her Directors' metrics after a snapshot is captured. As an `editor` who is an ancestor of her Director nodes, she has this capability.
- She cannot adjust a snapshot after the fact (that requires `owner` or `system_admin`). If post-quarter context warrants an adjustment, she brings it to Marcus.
- When she assigns a metric to a Director, the Director appears as the node owner and can co-author thresholds. Sarah reviews and approves.
