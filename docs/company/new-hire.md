# Persona: New Hire

## Aisha Torres

**Title:** Associate Product Manager  
**Company tenure:** 3 weeks  
**Direct reports:** None  
**System role:** `participant`  
**Node type:** `individual` (pending placement)  
**Reports to:** TBD — not yet resolved

---

## Who She Is

Aisha joined three weeks ago from a larger enterprise company. She is organized, eager, and unsettled by ambiguity. Her previous company used OKRs but the execution was inconsistent — goals were set in January and ignored by March. She wanted somewhere with more operational rigor.

She has been onboarded to the product and given access to Primer. She can see the org structure and has been introduced to the framework in her first week orientation. She does not yet have a node in the hierarchy — her `placement_request` is pending. HR knows she is there. Her hiring manager has been traveling. The placement has not been resolved.

Her experience in Primer right now is limited by this gap. She can see the system, understand the framework, and set up her profile — but she cannot fully participate until she has a node.

---

## What She Does in Primer (Once Placed)

- Completes her first metric and threshold calibration with her manager (a guided, collaborative process the first time through)
- Assigns weights to her metrics — likely leans heavily on manager guidance the first cycle
- Creates initial self-directed goals (mostly developmental at this stage: learn the product domain, complete compliance training, deliver first PM deliverable)
- Records performance data as her metrics activate
- Submits her metrics for manager review at cycle end

## What She Does in Primer Right Now (Pre-Placement)

- Views the org hierarchy to understand the company structure
- Reads through published metric names and goal structures for her peer team members (visibility permitting)
- Cannot create metrics, record performance, or participate in the inquiry process

---

## What She Cares About

- **Understanding what success looks like before her first review.** Aisha's primary professional anxiety as a new hire is not knowing what she is being evaluated against. The framework promises a documented, co-authored definition. She wants that conversation to happen quickly.
- **Getting placed.** Every day without a hierarchy node is a day she cannot fully participate. The placement request status is visible to HR and the owner. She wants it resolved.
- **Not being set up for failure.** Her first threshold calibration will expose gaps in her domain knowledge. She expects the process to be collaborative, not evaluative. Whether it feels that way depends on her manager.
- **Connection to the bigger picture.** She wants to see how her work connects to the goals above her in the hierarchy. The cascaded goal chain should be visible to her once placed.

---

## Pain Without the System

- In most companies, a new hire spends their first 90 days trying to figure out informally what success looks like. Nobody tells them explicitly. They read signals.
- Goal-setting, if it happens, usually happens weeks into the role and is disconnected from the operational framework the manager actually uses to evaluate performance.
- There is no visible path between her individual goals and the company's operational health. She cannot see how her work connects to anything above her.

---

## What Primer Resolves for Her

The onboarding path in Primer is structured: placement → metric assignment → threshold calibration → weight submission → first cycle. Each step requires a deliberate action from either Aisha or her manager. Nothing happens invisibly.

The cascaded goal structure means when her manager assigns her goals, she can see where they came from — which Director-level goal or VP-level goal they trace back to. She has context, not just instructions.

The framework gives her a documented answer to "what does good look like here" within her first month. That is the primary value for a new hire.

---

## Edge Cases

- Aisha's `placement_request` is visible to any `hr_admin` or `owner` in the system. It is not visible to `editor` or `participant` roles unless they have visibility grants.
- Until her node is created and her `user_id` is bound to it, she cannot own metrics, record performance, or file inquiries.
- Her first cycle will likely have `submitted_at` / `approved_at` gaps — metrics in draft state that need to complete the two-party review before the first snapshot. The manager should not capture a snapshot until the calibration is complete.
- If her manager is slow to place her, this is an HR escalation path: Linda (HR Director) has `hr_admin` access and can resolve the placement request directly.
